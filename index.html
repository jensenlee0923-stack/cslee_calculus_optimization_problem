Calculus of Courage

A Study on Duty, Risk, and Optimization

1. The Context: Tai Po Fire Incident

Incident Date: November 26, 2025

A massive No. 5 alarm fire engulfed Wang Fuk Court in Tai Po. Amidst the chaos of scaffolding ablaze and rapid smoke spread, firefighters faced the ultimate dilemma: the biological instinct for self-preservation versus the sworn duty to save lives. Tragically, a Senior Fireman gave his life during the operation.

In this task, we will explore how mathematical precision can optimize safety in dangerous environments, and reflect on the moral weight of the decisions first responders make.

2. The Problem: Optimizing the Rescue Path

Scenario: A firefighter enters a burning apartment at the door (Point A). A trapped resident is unconscious on the balcony (Point B). The apartment is split into two zones:

  • Zone 1 (Clear): The first 4 meters (y = 0 to y = 4) are relatively clear. Speed \( v_1 = 2 \, \text{m/s} \).
  • Zone 2 (Debris): The remaining distance (from y = 4 to y = 10) is filled with burning debris. Speed \( v_2 = 0.5 \, \text{m/s} \).

Geometry:

  • Point \( A \) is at coordinate \( (0, 0) \).
  • The boundary between Zone 1 and Zone 2 is the line \( y = 4 \).
  • Point \( B \) (the victim) is at coordinate \( (10, 10) \).
  • The firefighter must choose a point \( P(x, 4) \) on the boundary to cross into the debris zone.

Figure 1: Rescue path from Entry (A) to Balcony (B) crossing the boundary at P.

Your Task:

  1. Let \( T(x) \) be the total time taken to travel from \( A \) to \( B \) via point \( P(x, 4) \). Formulate the equation for \( T(x) \).
  2. Use differentiation (First Derivative Test) to find the value of \( x \) that minimizes the time \( T \).
  3. Calculate the minimum time required to reach the victim.
  4. (You also think about why this is the best path instead of walking from point A to point B directly)

3. The Dilemma: Duty vs. Self-Preservation

In the problem above, you used calculus to minimize exposure to danger (Time). In real life, however, firefighters often enter zones where the "calculated risk" is extremely high, defying the instinct for self-preservation to fulfill their duty.

"Calculus can help us find the safest path, but it cannot explain why someone chooses to take the path at all."

Reflecting on the Tai Po fire and the loss of the firefighter, write a short response (50 words) discussing how society should balance the expectation of duty from our first responders against their right to self-preservation.
