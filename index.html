1. The Context: Tai Po Fire Incident

Incident Date: November 26, 2025

During the massive fire at Wang Fuk Court, firefighters had to make split-second decisions. In this task, we analyze a scenario where "Calculus meets Courage." We will use the Factor Theorem and Differentiation to find the optimal path that minimizes danger.

2. The Problem: Integer Optimization

Scenario: A firefighter starts at the door A(0,0) and must reach a victim at B(7,7). The room is divided into two zones by the line y=3.

  • Zone 1 (Clear): Height of 3m. The firefighter runs fast at \( v_1 = 4 \, \text{m/s} \).
  • Zone 2 (Debris): Height of 4m. The firefighter is slowed down to \( v_2 = 3 \, \text{m/s} \).

Geometry:

  • Point \( A \) is at \( (0, 0) \).
  • Boundary line is \( y = 3 \).
  • Point \( B \) is at \( (7, 7) \).
  • The firefighter crosses the boundary at point \( P(x, 3) \).

Your Task:

  1. Formulate the time equation \( T(x) \).
  2. Differentiate \( T(x) \) and set \( T'(x) = 0 \).
  3. Show that the optimization condition simplifies to the polynomial equation: $$ x^4 - 14x^3 + 49x^2 - 288x + 1008 = 0 $$
  4. Use the Factor Theorem to check integer values (Hint: Try small integers) to find the optimal path \( x \).

3. The Dilemma: Duty vs. Self-Preservation
